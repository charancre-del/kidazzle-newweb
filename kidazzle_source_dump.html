The Creative Curriculum¬Æ

A research-based, whole-child approach to education that adapts to every environment.

Research-Based & Child-Centered

At KIDazzle, we don't just "watch" children. We engage them. Using the nationally recognized Creative Curriculum¬Æ, we balance teacher-directed and child-initiated learning.

38 Objectives for Development

The curriculum identifies goals in all four major areas of development: Social/Emotional, Physical, Cognitive, and Language. Teachers use these goals to plan activities that are relevant to the children's lives.

The Interest Areas

Classrooms are divided into interest areas: Blocks, Dramatic Play, Toys & Games, Art, Library, Discovery, Sand & Water, and Music & Movement. This layout promotes independence.

Meeting State Standards

Georgia (GELDS & Frog Street)

For our Georgia centers, specifically our GA Pre-K classrooms, we utilize Frog Street in conjunction with the Georgia Early Learning and Development Standards (GELDS). This ensures rigorous preparation for the K-12 system.

Tennessee (TN-ELDS)

Our Memphis location adheres to the Tennessee Early Learning Developmental Standards (TN-ELDS), ensuring that our curriculum supports the state's specific educational goals for birth to age 5.

Florida (Early Learning Standards)

In Doral, our curriculum is aligned with the Florida Early Learning and Developmental Standards, preparing children for VPK and beyond with a focus on literacy and mathematical thinking.

Adapting to Our Environment

One of the strengths of Creative Curriculum is its flexibility. At KIDazzle, we integrate the environment into the lesson plans:

  • Urban Classrooms (Downtown Atlanta/Memphis): We use the cityscape as a teaching tool‚Äîexploring architecture, transportation, and community helpers in the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Suburban/Rural Settings (Hampton/College Park): We leverage larger outdoor spaces for nature walks, gardening projects, and environmental science exploration.
